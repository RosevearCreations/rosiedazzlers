Why this page exists

These special-service pages are meant to stand on their own. They give the service enough room for process, equipment, expectations, proof, and clear reasons to book or ask for a quote.

Paint correction is condition-based work. Swirls, oxidation, wash marring, and deeper defects all change the right polishing approach and the time required.

Process and preparation

  • wash and decontamination before polishing is considered
  • paint-condition review to judge defect level and realistic improvement
  • single-stage or heavier polishing path based on the finish
  • protection recommendation after correction work is complete

A dedicated service page should look like a real service page. That means process, visuals, equipment context, and clear expectations instead of a thin placeholder.

Equipment and workflow

  • inspection lighting for defect visibility
  • machine-polishing workflow matched to paint condition
  • surface prep process before polishing starts
  • follow-up protection recommendations after correction

Best fit for

  • owners chasing a glossier finish
  • vehicles with swirl marks or dull paint
  • customers pairing correction with sealant, graphene, or ceramic protection

Common questions

Is paint correction always a two-stage polish?

Not always. The right level depends on the condition of the paint and the result being chased.

Do you need exterior detailing first?

Yes. The paint has to be properly cleaned and prepared first.

Can correction be combined with sealant, graphene, or ceramic services?

Yes. Many customers protect the finish after correction work is done.
