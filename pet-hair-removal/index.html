Why this page exists

These special-service pages are meant to stand on their own. They give the service enough room for process, equipment, expectations, proof, and clear reasons to book or ask for a quote.

Heavy pet-hair jobs can take much longer than a standard vacuum pass, especially when hair is worked into woven fabric, trunk liner, or seat seams.

Process and preparation

  • inspection of the worst pet-hair zones before work begins
  • targeted agitation, brushing, and repeat extraction where needed
  • extra attention around seat rails, cargo areas, and fabric seams
  • recommendation of the right base interior package when the job needs more than a quick clean

A dedicated service page should look like a real service page. That means process, visuals, equipment context, and clear expectations instead of a thin placeholder.

Equipment and workflow

  • specialty brushes and agitation tools
  • vacuum and extraction workflow for embedded hair zones
  • interior-safe products for plastics, trim, and finished surfaces
  • lighting checks so missed hair along edges can be caught

Best fit for

  • family vehicles with dogs or cats
  • used vehicles being cleaned up for resale
  • repeat customers who want a focused interior reset instead of a light vacuum

Common questions

Can pet hair removal be booked by itself?

Sometimes, but heavy jobs often work best when paired with an interior or complete detail.

Why can pricing vary?

Hair type, seat fabric, and how deeply the hair is embedded can change labour time.

Do you work cargo areas and floor mats too?

Yes. Cargo liners, floor mats, seat seams, and tight corners are often where the hardest hair sits.
